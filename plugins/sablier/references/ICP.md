# Sablier Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

## Primary Audiences

### 1. DAO Treasury Managers & Operations

**Description**: People responsible for managing DAO treasuries and distributing tokens to contributors, investors,
and community members.

**Demographics:**

- Roles: Treasury Lead, Operations Manager, DAO Contributor, Multisig Signer
- Org types: Protocol DAOs, Investment DAOs, Social DAOs, Service DAOs
- Experience: Familiar with crypto, may or may not be deeply technical

**Pain Points:**

- Manual token distributions are time-consuming and error-prone
- Spreadsheet tracking of vesting schedules
- Multisig approval overhead for recurring payments
- No visibility into distribution schedules for recipients
- Security concerns with custom vesting contracts

**Goals:**

- Automate token distribution (set and forget)
- Reduce operational overhead
- Provide transparency to recipients
- Ensure security of treasury funds
- Align incentives with long-term thinking

**What Resonates:**

- Time savings and automation
- Security track record (no hacks)
- Decentralization (no trust required)
- Real-time access for recipients
- Low, predictable fees (senders can cover for recipients)

### 2. Token Launchers & Founders

**Description**: Teams launching new tokens who need to distribute to team, investors, advisors, and community.

**Demographics:**

- Roles: Founder, CEO, CTO, Token Economics Lead
- Org types: DeFi protocols, Infrastructure projects, NFT projects, L2s/L3s
- Stage: Pre-launch to early growth

**Pain Points:**

- Day-1 dumping from airdrop recipients
- Short-term interest that fades after token launch
- Complex vesting schedules to implement
- Security risks with custom smart contracts
- High gas costs for large distributions

**Goals:**

- Prevent immediate selling pressure
- Create long-term community alignment
- Professional token distribution infrastructure
- Minimize technical overhead
- Establish credibility with investors

**What Resonates:**

- Airstreams preventing day-1 dumps
- Battle-tested security (don't roll your own)
- Scalability (50k recipients per campaign)
- Gas efficiency (recipients pay claim gas)
- Used by major protocols (social proof)
- Option for senders to cover recipient fees

### 3. Crypto-Native Companies

**Description**: Startups and companies that pay contributors in crypto and need payroll infrastructure.

**Demographics:**

- Roles: HR/People Ops, Finance Lead, Founder
- Org types: Crypto startups, Remote-first companies, Protocol teams
- Size: 5-100 people, often distributed globally

**Pain Points:**

- Manual monthly/bi-weekly token transfers
- No real-time access for employees
- Spreadsheet tracking
- Timezone and scheduling complexity
- Contributor visibility into compensation

**Goals:**

- Continuous, automated salary streaming
- Real-time compensation access for contributors
- Reduced administrative burden
- Professional payroll infrastructure

**What Resonates:**

- Money streaming concept (get paid by the second)
- Set-and-forget automation
- Great UX for both employers and employees
- Integration with existing treasury setup (Safe)

### 4. Developers & Integrators

**Description**: Technical builders who integrate Sablier into their own products or want to understand the protocol.

**Demographics:**

- Roles: Smart Contract Developer, Full-Stack Developer, Protocol Engineer
- Context: Building products that need token distribution, vesting, or streaming

**Pain Points:**

- Building custom vesting contracts is risky and time-consuming
- Need reliable, audited infrastructure to build on
- Documentation quality varies across protocols

**Goals:**

- Integrate proven infrastructure instead of building from scratch
- Understand how the protocol works at a technical level
- Ship faster with less risk

**What Resonates:**

- Open-source, well-documented code
- Multiple audits
- API and SDK availability
- Technical blog posts and examples
- Active developer community

## Secondary Audiences

### Crypto Media & Analysts

Journalists, researchers, analysts covering DeFi infrastructure.

**What they need**: Clear explanations, data points, unique angles, expert commentary

### Investors

VCs, angels, institutional investors evaluating DeFi infrastructure.

**What they need**: Traction metrics, competitive positioning, team credibility, market size

## Audience Language

### Terms They Use

vesting, unlock schedule, cliff, airdrop, token distribution, treasury management, multisig, Safe, governance, DAO,
TVL, smart contract, gas fees, on-chain

### Questions They Ask

- "How do I prevent my airdrop from being dumped immediately?"
- "Is this more secure than building our own vesting contract?"
- "How much does it cost?"
- "Can I integrate this with our Safe multisig?"
- "What happens if Sablier disappears?"
- "How do recipients claim their tokens?"
- "Can we customize the vesting curve?"

### Objections They Raise

- "We can build this ourselves"
- "What if we need custom functionality?"
- "What are the fees exactly?"
- "We're concerned about smart contract risk"
- "Our legal team has questions about token streaming"
